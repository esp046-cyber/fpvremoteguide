Understanding Your FPV Remote

An FPV drone remote control (also called a transmitter or radio) is your primary interface for controlling your drone. Modern FPV remotes operate on 2.4GHz frequency and use protocols like ELRS, Crossfire, or Tracer.

Common Remote Types

  • RadioMaster TX16S: Popular multi-protocol radio with OpenTX/EdgeTX
  • RadioMaster Zorro: Compact gamepad-style controller
  • TBS Tango 2: Crossfire integrated compact radio
  • Jumper T-Lite: Budget-friendly lightweight option
  • FrSky Taranis: Classic choice with AccSt protocol

Key Components

  • Gimbals: Two joysticks that control throttle, yaw, pitch, and roll
  • Switches: Toggle and momentary switches for flight modes and arming
  • Display Screen: Shows telemetry, settings, and model information
  • Antenna: Transmits control signals to your drone
  • Battery: Powers the remote (usually 2S LiPo or 18650 cells)

Stick Controls Explained

Mode 2 (Most Common in FPV)

This guide uses Mode 2 configuration, which is the standard for FPV and drone racing worldwide.

Left Stick

YAW RIGHT YAW LEFT THROTTLE DOWN THROTTLE UP

Up/Down: Throttle (altitude)

Left/Right: Yaw (rotation)

Right Stick

PITCH FORWARD PITCH BACK ROLL LEFT ROLL RIGHT

Up/Down: Pitch (forward/back)

Left/Right: Roll (strafe left/right)

Control Movements Explained

  • Throttle: Controls motor speed and altitude. Push up to ascend, down to descend
  • Yaw: Rotates the drone left or right around its center axis
  • Pitch: Tips the drone forward or backward to move in that direction
  • Roll: Tips the drone left or right to strafe sideways
üí° Pro Tip: Most FPV pilots use "pinch" grip (thumb and index finger) for precise control, especially on the right stick.

Setting Up Your Remote

Charge Your Remote

Fully charge the remote battery before first use. Most remotes use 2S LiPo (7.4V) or dual 18650 cells.

Install RF Module

If using external module (ELRS, Crossfire), install it in the module bay on the back of the radio. Secure the antenna properly.

Create a Model

Navigate to the model menu and create a new model profile. Name it after your drone (e.g., "5inch Freestyle").

Bind to Receiver

Put your drone's receiver in bind mode, then activate bind mode on your transmitter. Wait for solid LED indicating successful binding.

Configure Channels

Map channels: CH1=Roll, CH2=Pitch, CH3=Throttle, CH4=Yaw. Assign switches for Arm (CH5) and Flight Mode (CH6).

Set Failsafe

Configure failsafe to drop throttle to zero if signal is lost. This prevents flyaways.

Calibrate in Betaflight

Connect to Betaflight Configurator, go to Receiver tab, and verify all stick inputs show correct ranges (1000-2000).

Flight Modes

üî∞ ANGLE Mode

Best for: Beginners

Auto-levels the drone when you release the sticks. Prevents flips and limits tilt angle. Great for learning basic controls.

üåä HORIZON Mode

Best for: Intermediate pilots

Auto-levels at small stick inputs but allows flips at full stick deflection. Good transition mode.

üöÄ ACRO Mode

Best for: FPV and racing

Full manual control with no auto-leveling. Stick position controls rotation rate. Required for advanced freestyle and racing.

‚ö° AIR Mode

Always on in FPV

Keeps PID controller active at zero throttle. Essential for FPV flying, allows control during flips and rolls.

Configuring Flight Mode Switch

Most pilots use a 3-position switch for flight modes:

  • Position 1: Angle mode (safe)
  • Position 2: Horizon mode (mixed)
  • Position 3: Acro mode (full manual)
üí° Progression Tip: Start with Angle mode for your first flights. Once comfortable, try Horizon mode. Move to Acro when you can confidently control orientation.

Before Every Flight

  • Check radio battery level (above 30%)
  • Verify correct model is selected
  • Test all switches and stick movements
  • Confirm arm switch is in disarmed position
  • Check RF link quality in OSD

Gimbal Calibration

Calibrate your gimbals if you notice drift or incorrect centering:

  • Access radio settings menu
  • Find calibration option
  • Move both sticks through full range of motion
  • Center both sticks and confirm
  • Test in Betaflight receiver tab

Common Switch Assignments

Arm Switch

2-position switch (usually SA or SB). Down=Disarmed, Up=Armed. Always disarm when done flying.

Flight Mode

3-position switch (usually SC or SD). Maps to Angle, Horizon, and Acro modes.

Beeper

Momentary switch (usually SE). Triggers lost model alarm to find crashed drone.

Turtle Mode

Momentary switch. Reverses motors to flip drone right-side up after crash.

üîß Troubleshooting Common Issues

‚ùå Problem: Radio won't bind to receiver

Symptoms: Receiver LED blinking, no connection in OSD

‚úÖ Solutions:

  • Ensure receiver is in bind mode (usually hold button while powering on)
  • Check that radio and receiver use same protocol (ELRS, Crossfire, etc.)
  • Update firmware on both radio and receiver to matching versions
  • Try re-flashing receiver firmware via Betaflight passthrough
  • Check binding phrase matches (for ELRS)

‚ùå Problem: Poor range or signal dropouts

Symptoms: Failsafe triggering, low RSSI/LQ, control stuttering

‚úÖ Solutions:

  • Check transmitter and receiver antennas are properly connected
  • Ensure antennas are perpendicular (not parallel) to each other
  • Verify transmitter power level is set correctly (check local regulations)
  • Check for carbon fiber blocking receiver antenna (relocate if needed)
  • Update to latest firmware - often improves link quality
  • Try different packet rate (e.g., 250Hz vs 500Hz for ELRS)

‚ùå Problem: Stick drift or incorrect centering

Symptoms: Drone drifts when sticks are centered, incorrect values in Betaflight

‚úÖ Solutions:

  • Calibrate gimbals in radio settings menu
  • Clean gimbal potentiometers with contact cleaner
  • Adjust gimbal tension (too loose can cause drift)
  • Check for physical damage to gimbal mechanism
  • Set proper endpoints in radio (usually 1000-2000)
  • May need gimbal replacement if severely worn

‚ùå Problem: Can't arm the drone

Symptoms: Arm switch doesn't activate motors, beeping from drone

‚úÖ Solutions:

  • Check arm channel is assigned correctly (usually CH5)
  • Verify accelerometer is calibrated in Betaflight
  • Check CLI tab for arming prevention flags
  • Ensure throttle is at zero position
  • Disable "Airmode" for initial arming if required
  • Check if failsafe mode is active (bad receiver connection)
  • Verify battery voltage is sufficient (above 3.5V per cell)

‚ùå Problem: Wrong control response (inverted or mixed channels)

Symptoms: Pushing forward makes drone go backward, roll controls yaw, etc.

‚úÖ Solutions:

  • Check channel mapping in Betaflight receiver tab (AETR order)
  • Verify channel assignments match: CH1=Roll, CH2=Pitch, CH3=Throttle, CH4=Yaw
  • Use Betaflight channel map setting if channels are in wrong order
  • Check for reversed channels - tick "Reverse" boxes as needed
  • Ensure correct model selected on transmitter

‚ùå Problem: Radio battery drains quickly

Symptoms: Battery only lasts 2-3 hours instead of 8+

‚úÖ Solutions:

  • Lower backlight brightness in radio settings
  • Disable haptic feedback if not needed
  • Check battery health (may need replacement if old)
  • Ensure radio is actually turning off (not going to sleep mode)
  • Reduce RF power if using high power unnecessarily
  • Update firmware - newer versions often have better power management

‚ùå Problem: Telemetry not working

Symptoms: No battery voltage, RSSI, or other data on radio screen

‚úÖ Solutions:

  • Enable telemetry in Betaflight configuration
  • Check telemetry protocol matches (SmartPort, CRSF, etc.)
  • Verify telemetry wire connected properly to flight controller
  • Set up telemetry screens in radio (may need manual configuration)
  • Update receiver firmware - older versions may have bugs

üìö FPV & Radio Terminology

Essential terms every FPV pilot should know:

Radio/Transmitter Terms

TX (Transmitter)

Your radio controller that sends signals to the drone. Also called the radio or remote.

RX (Receiver)

The component on your drone that receives signals from the transmitter.

Gimbal

The joystick mechanism on your radio. Contains potentiometers to detect stick position.

OpenTX / EdgeTX

Open-source operating systems for radio transmitters. EdgeTX is a fork of OpenTX with more features.

ELRS (ExpressLRS)

Open-source, long-range, low-latency RC link. Very popular for FPV racing and freestyle.

Crossfire (TBS)

Long-range RC link system by Team BlackSheep. Known for reliability and range up to 40km.

Binding

Process of pairing your transmitter with your receiver so they communicate securely.

Failsafe

Safety feature that controls what happens when radio signal is lost (usually disarms and drops).

Telemetry

Data sent back from drone to radio (battery voltage, RSSI, GPS, etc.).

Channel

Individual control input. Standard setup uses 4+ channels (Throttle, Yaw, Pitch, Roll, Arm, Mode, etc.).

Signal & Connection Terms

RSSI

Received Signal Strength Indicator. Shows how strong the radio signal is (higher = better).

LQ (Link Quality)

Percentage of successfully received packets. 100% = perfect, below 70% = concerning.

Latency

Delay between moving stick and drone responding. Lower is better. ELRS can achieve ~4ms.

Packet Rate

How many times per second radio sends updates (e.g., 150Hz, 250Hz, 500Hz). Higher = lower latency but shorter range.

2.4GHz / 900MHz

Radio frequencies. 2.4GHz is standard, 900MHz has better range but fewer channels and needs license in some regions.

Flight Control Terms

Throttle

Controls motor speed/altitude. Up = more power, down = less power.

Yaw

Rotation around vertical axis. Left stick left/right on Mode 2.

Pitch

Forward/backward tilt. Right stick up/down on Mode 2.

Roll

Left/right tilt. Right stick left/right on Mode 2.

Rates

How sensitive stick inputs are. Higher rates = more aggressive response.

Expo (Exponential)

Reduces sensitivity at center stick, increases at edges. Makes flying smoother.

Acro Mode

Manual flight mode with no auto-leveling. Stick position controls rotation rate.

Angle Mode

Self-leveling mode. Drone returns to level when sticks centered. Good for beginners.

Horizon Mode

Hybrid mode. Auto-levels at small inputs, allows flips at full stick.

Air Mode

Keeps stabilization active at zero throttle. Essential for acrobatic flying.

Turtle Mode

Reverses motors to flip drone upside-right after crashing upside-down.

Arming

Activating motors to prepare for flight. Always disarm when not flying.

General FPV Terms

OSD (On-Screen Display)

Information overlay in FPV goggles (battery voltage, timer, RSSI, etc.).

VTX (Video Transmitter)

Sends video from drone camera to your goggles. Operates on 5.8GHz.

FC (Flight Controller)

Brain of the drone. Runs Betaflight/KISS/etc. and controls motors.

ESC (Electronic Speed Controller)

Controls individual motor speeds based on FC commands.

PID

Proportional-Integral-Derivative tuning values that control how drone responds to inputs.

Betaflight

Most popular flight controller firmware. Open-source and highly configurable.

LiPo (Lithium Polymer)

Battery type used in FPV. Measured in cells (1S, 2S, 4S, 6S, etc.). Higher = more voltage/power.

C Rating

Battery discharge rate. Higher C rating = can deliver more current safely.

mAh (Milliamp Hours)

Battery capacity. Higher mAh = longer flight time but heavier.

DVR

Digital Video Recording. Records FPV footage from goggles (lower quality than HD cam).
